Klefe New School Project

As part of activities to earmark Adanu’s 20th Anniversary celebrations, Klefe, a community in the Ho Central Municipality, has been selected as a beneficiary of a 3-Unit Classroom block.

In line with Adanu’s advocacy for sustainable community development, the team led by Mypa Buckner, Director of Operations, gave a brief background on the undertakings of the organisation at its first Stakeholder engagement meeting with the community.



The Director of Construction, Angelbert Ankrah, also gave a breakdown on the project, which comprises a 3-Unit classroom with an office and a storeroom. He further reiterated the role the community must play in order for the project to commence, which is for them to bring on-deck 30% of skilled and unskilled labour together with some construction materials.

Furthermore, the community expressed their profound gratitude to the organisation and pledged their utmost commitment towards the project, with both parties agreeing to cut sod for the commencement of project upon the community’s acquisition of 90% of their portion.

Visit to Kpordui

The Adanu team has recently made a site visit to its ongoing project at the Kpordui D/A Basic School, in the Anloga district of the Volta Region, to ascertain progress made since it's commencement.

The project, which began late April, 2022, comprised a 3-Unit Classroom with an office and store.

Although the project is challenged with the rainy season, it has seen 60% of construction works.



Nonetheless, we look forward to having the project completed in time for its commissioning in August this year. 

Meanwhile, the Directors of Construction and Operations, in a brief engagement with community members present at the site for filling works, commended them and further entreated them to show more commitment towards the project than they are doing currently.

Adanu Online Auction

As part of activities for the upcoming 20th anniversary celebrations, Friends of Adanu successfully hosted an online auction sale to raise funding for ongoing build projects in the Volta Region of Ghana.



The items auctioned, which were from Ghana, included, but were not limited to, bags, fabrics, beads, and crafts. The entire Adanu team would like to thank each and everyone who wholeheartedly participated in this auction sale. Your willingness to see us inspire communities together is heartwarming.
